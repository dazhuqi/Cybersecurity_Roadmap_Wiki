This directory manages the learning process rather than one specific technical topic. The technical notes explain cybersecurity concepts. The meta notes help me decide what to study, what to review, and where my weak spots are.

Beginner version:

> This folder is the control panel for the wiki.

## How I Should Use This Folder

- Start the week in [progress-tracker.md](progress-tracker.md).
- Use [learning-log.md](learning-log.md) after each study session.
- Put recurring terms into [glossary.md](glossary.md).
- End the week with [weekly-review.md](weekly-review.md).
- Update [wiki-structure.md](wiki-structure.md) when new folders or rules are added.

## Weekly Maintenance Routine

1. Check the progress tracker.
2. Pick one current module focus.
3. Review unfinished notes.
4. Move unclear terms into the glossary.
5. Turn at least one lab or reading session into a concrete note.
6. Write the weekly review.

## What Good Tracking Looks Like

Good tracking is short but honest:

- what I studied
- what I actually practiced
- what is still confusing
- what I will do next

Bad tracking is just a list of resources I opened. Opening a page is not the same as learning it.

## Rules for Future Me

- Do not mark a topic complete if I cannot explain it without reading.
- Do not hide confusion. Confusion is useful data.
- Do not collect five new resources when one practice task would help more.
- Link notes together when topics overlap.
- Keep templates practical and easy to fill.

## Maintenance Boundaries

This folder should stay boring in a good way. It is here to make studying easier, not to become another project.

If I spend more time reorganizing trackers than learning, I should stop and choose one concrete task:

- rewrite one confusing note
- finish one small lab
- review one glossary section
- close one unused resource
- explain one concept out loud

The best version of this folder is not fancy. It is the version I actually update.
