## Notes

GIAC is a certification body connected with the SANS training ecosystem. GIAC certifications cover many areas: security essentials, incident response, forensics, penetration testing, cloud, management, industrial control systems, and more.

For my roadmap, GIAC is a "large ecosystem" page. The specific certifications in this repo are GSEC, GPEN, and GWAPT, but I should understand that GIAC has many more options.

## Why It Matters for My Roadmap

GIAC certifications can be valuable because they tend to map to focused security skill areas. The downside is that SANS/GIAC paths can be expensive, so I should be realistic and not treat them as casual impulse exams.

This page helps me compare:

- [GSEC](gsec.md): security essentials
- [GPEN](gpen.md): penetration testing
- [GWAPT](gwapt.md): web application penetration testing

## How I Should Think About GIAC

- Choose a GIAC cert only after I know the role direction.
- Check whether the target job or employer values that specific certification.
- Use the certification objectives as a study map even if I do not register.
- Prefer hands-on understanding over collecting names.
- Compare cost, renewal, training access, and alternatives.

## Common GIAC Directions

| Direction | Example Focus |
| --- | --- |
