## Notes

CEH stands for Certified Ethical Hacker. It is an EC-Council certification focused on ethical hacking concepts, attack phases, tools, and common offensive security topics.

I should treat CEH carefully. It can be useful as a broad overview or for job filters in some regions, but practical skill still needs hands-on labs, careful methodology, and real writeups.

## Why It Matters for My Roadmap

CEH-style study connects to:

- reconnaissance concepts
- scanning and enumeration
- vulnerability analysis
- web application basics
- social engineering awareness
- malware and system hacking terminology
- reporting and rules of engagement

The most important thing is authorization. Ethical hacking is only ethical inside a clear legal scope.

## What I Should Learn From It

- The basic phases of an authorized security test.
- Common tools and what questions they answer.
- Difference between scanning, enumeration, exploitation, and post-exploitation.
- Web, wireless, cloud, and mobile security terminology.
- Basic reporting language.
- Legal and ethical boundaries.

## How I Would Prepare

1. Learn security foundations first, especially networking and operating systems.
2. Practice only in authorized labs such as TryHackMe, Hack The Box, or local vulnerable VMs.
3. Write down methodology instead of only saving tool commands.
4. Build a habit of writing scope, evidence, risk, and remediation.
5. Compare CEH objectives with my practical lab notes before deciding to register.

## Hands-On Practice Ideas

- Complete beginner rooms on reconnaissance and enumeration.
- Practice `nmap` on lab targets only.
- Write a sample rules-of-engagement note for a fake lab.
- Turn one solved lab into a short report with findings and fixes.
- Review basic web vulnerabilities in a safe training environment.
