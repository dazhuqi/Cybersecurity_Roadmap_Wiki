## Notes

CISA stands for Certified Information Systems Auditor. It is focused on audit, assurance, governance, risk, and control. This is not a hands-on hacking certification. It is more about checking whether systems, processes, and controls are designed and operating properly.

For my roadmap, CISA is useful to know about because cybersecurity is not only technical defense. Organizations also need audits, evidence, policies, risk decisions, and compliance work.

## Why It Matters for My Roadmap

CISA-style knowledge helps with:

- understanding IT audits
- mapping controls to business risk
- reviewing governance and management processes
- checking evidence instead of only trusting statements
- understanding compliance-driven security work

This is probably not my first certification unless I move toward audit or governance work.

## What I Should Learn From It

- Information systems audit process.
- Governance and management of IT.
- Systems acquisition, development, and implementation.
- Information systems operations and business resilience.
- Protection of information assets.
- Evidence, findings, and control testing.

## How I Would Prepare

1. Learn basic security controls first.
2. Read about risk, governance, audit evidence, and control frameworks.
3. Practice writing control observations in plain language.
4. Study official ISACA exam information before choosing materials.
5. Talk to audit or GRC practitioners if possible.

## Practice Ideas

- Pick one control, such as MFA, and write how I would test it.
- Compare policy language with actual technical evidence.
- Write a mock audit finding with risk, evidence, and recommendation.
- Review a framework such as NIST CSF or CIS Controls at a high level.
