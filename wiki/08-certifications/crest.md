## Notes

CREST is a professional certification and accreditation body often associated with penetration testing, security assessment, incident response, and related assurance work. The exact value of CREST certifications can depend on region, employer, and role.

For me, CREST is something to understand as part of the professional pentesting landscape. It may matter more if I target roles or markets where CREST is commonly requested.

## Why It Matters for My Roadmap

CREST-style certification paths are connected to:

- professional security assessment
- penetration testing methodology
- report writing
- client scope and rules of engagement
- practical technical depth
- ethics and professional standards

It is not just about "using tools." Professional assessment work requires clean scope, evidence, communication, and repeatable process.

## What I Should Learn From It

- How professional penetration testing roles are structured.
- Difference between junior, practitioner, and advanced assessment expectations.
- Why reporting quality matters.
- How methodology and evidence support findings.
- Why legal scope and client communication are part of technical work.

## How I Would Prepare

1. Read the official CREST certification pages and identify which path is relevant.
2. Build strong networking, web, Linux, and Windows fundamentals.
3. Complete authorized labs and write clean reports.
4. Compare CREST with OSCP, GPEN, and other practical paths.
5. Check whether local job postings actually ask for CREST.

## Practice Ideas

- Write a short penetration test report for a lab machine.
- Practice explaining risk and remediation clearly.
- Build a methodology checklist for reconnaissance, enumeration, exploitation, and reporting.
- Review sample professional report structures.
